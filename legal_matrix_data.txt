Arizona
A commercial entity that knowingly and intentionally publishes or distributes material on an internet website, including a social media platform, of which more than one-third is sexual material that is harmful to minors shall use reasonable age verification methods to verify that an individual who attempts to access the material is eighteen years of age or older. 
Must perform age verification by:
-providing a form of digital ID that does not cause the information to be transmitted to a governmental entity
-Use an age verification system that utilizes government-issued IDs or -a commercially reasonable method that relies on public and private transactional data
$10,000 per day that website is operated in violation of the law; or
$10,000 per instance where personal information is retained for non-verification purposes.

AND

Up to $250,000 if one or more minors accesses sexual materials harmful to minors

Identity Assurance Level 2 is a framework for confirming an individual’s ownership of a genuine identity by using personal information, identity documentation, and biometric characteristics

Websites with harmful to minor content must verify the age of individuals before it can be accessed
Must offer two methods:

-Anonymous age verification which does not require user to disclose PI

Georgia
Websites are required to use "reasonable age verification methods" to confirm that individuals accessing the material are at least 18 years old
Acceptable methods include: 
-Submission of a digitized identification card, such as a digital copy of a driver's license. 
-Submission of government-issued identification. 
-Any commercially reasonable age verification method meeting or exceeding an Identity Assurance Level 2 
$10,000 for each violation

1 year statute of limitations

Yes
South Dakota
Covered platforms must implement reasonable age verification.
Verification includes using:
- a state-issued driver’s license or non-driver card
-individual’s bank account information
-debit or credit card from the individual that requires the individual in ownership of the card to be at least eighteen years of age; or
-Any other method or document that reliably and accurately indicates if a user of a covered platform is a minor
$5,000 for each separate instance where reasonable verification is not performed.
HB 1053
Yes
Tennessee
Commercial entities that publish or distribute content that is harmful to minors are liable if the individual or commercial entity does not conduct verification methods.
Verification methods must not be easily bypassed or circumvented and must include:
-matching photographs of the active user; or 
-a commercially reasonable method relying on public or private transactional data to verify a person’s identity

Anonymized age-verification data must be retained for 7 years.
Liability includes court costs and reasonable attorney fees.

-driver’s license
